# Customer Journey Map for Code-Scout

## 1. Aware
- **Trigger Event**: Tech professionals encounter limitations with existing search and analytics tools that compromise privacy and efficiency.
- **Friction Points**: Lack of awareness about local-first solutions; existing tools are entrenched in the market.
- **User Emotions**: Frustration, curiosity, skepticism about new solutions.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Educational content highlighting the benefits of local-first and private solutions; engaging marketing campaigns showcasing success stories.
- **Metric**: Number of impressions and engagement rates on awareness campaigns.

## 2. Consider
- **Trigger Event**: Users begin researching alternatives after recognizing the limitations of their current tools.
- **Friction Points**: Overwhelmed by choices; difficulty in comparing features and benefits; concerns about data security.
- **User Emotions**: Hopeful yet cautious; desire for reassurance about the product's effectiveness and security.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Clear comparison charts; testimonials from trusted industry figures; free webinars or demos to showcase capabilities.
- **Metric**: Number of website visits and demo requests.

## 3. Try
- **Trigger Event**: Users decide to test Code-Scout through a free trial or demo.
- **Friction Points**: Initial setup complexity; potential technical issues during trial; lack of immediate support or guidance.
- **User Emotions**: Excitement mixed with anxiety about usability; fear of wasting time if the tool doesn't meet expectations.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Streamlined onboarding process; responsive customer support; interactive tutorials or guides to facilitate ease of use.
- **Metric**: Trial conversion rate and user feedback scores.

## 4. Adopt
- **Trigger Event**: Users successfully integrate Code-Scout into their workflow and recognize its value.
- **Friction Points**: Resistance to change from established workflows; potential integration issues with existing tools.
- **User Emotions**: Satisfaction from improved efficiency; relief from enhanced privacy; pride in adopting a cutting-edge solution.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Personalized follow-up support; advanced feature training sessions; community engagement through forums or user groups.
- **Metric**: User retention rate and frequency of use metrics.

## 5. Expand
- **Trigger Event**: Users begin to explore additional features or recommend Code-Scout to colleagues.
- **Friction Points**: Unawareness of all available features; potential limitations in scalability or collaboration.
- **User Emotions**: Enthusiasm for sharing a valuable tool; desire for continuous improvement and innovation.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Regular updates on new features; referral incentives; exclusive access to beta features for loyal users.
- **Metric**: Net Promoter Score (NPS) and number of referrals generated.
